CoG2 RH-LLM: A Geometric Graph Attention Framework for Faithful Knowledge-Grounded
Dialogue Generation
Xinzhi Wang, Yuxuan Li, Ruishen Liu, Mingxuan Wang, Xiangfeng Luo


   • Propose a novel CoG2 RH-LLM framework to mitigate factual hallucinations in knowledge-intensive domain.

   • Design a geometric graph attention for fine-grained, structure-aware KG fusion.
   • Introduce graph-context contrastive learning to enforce semantic consistency.
   • The model outperforms strong baselines including RHO on the OpenDialKG benchmark.

ARTICLE INFO                                        ABSTRACT
Keywords:                                           Factual hallucination is a significant challenge in Large Language Models, limiting their practical
Large Language Models                               applications for dialogue generation in specific, knowledge-intensive domains. A key mitigation strategy
Knowledge Graphs                                    involves grounding dialogues in Knowledge Graphs, which provide reliable structured facts. However,
Hallucination Reduction                             this approach is constrained by two primary issues: structural heterogeneity and information noise. To
Knowledge-Enhanced Dialogue                         address these challenges, this paper proposes a novel geometric graph attention framework to enhance
Graph Attention Networks                            dialogue fidelity. First, the framework addresses structural heterogeneity using a Geometric Weighted
                                                    Average Graph Attention mechanism. It achieves precise fusion by applying component-wise attention
                                                    to deconstructed KG triples, followed by a geometric mean aggregation. Second, a knowledge gating
                                                    mechanism mitigates information noise by dynamically controlling knowledge integration to filter
                                                    irrelevant facts. Finally, a graph-context contrastive learning objective aligns output representations
                                                    with the relevant KG subgraph to enforce semantic consistency and ensure robust factual grounding.
                                                    Experiments on the OpenDialKG dataset, a benchmark grounded in specific domains including movies,
                                                    music, and sports, confirm the framework’s effectiveness, showing significant outperformance over
                                                    strong baselines. A 6.14% improvement in ROUGE-L demonstrates effective noise suppression, as the
                                                    model’s outputs align more closely with ground-truth responses. Furthermore, a high Entity Coverage
                                                    Precision of 97.53% indicates the framework’s ability to resolve structural heterogeneity, confirming
                                                    the accuracy of its fine-grained fusion. The model’s factual consistency is further corroborated by a
                                                    3.60% gain on QuestEval and superior human evaluations. These evaluations affirmed its ability to
                                                    generate coherent and relevant responses while mitigating hallucinations.
